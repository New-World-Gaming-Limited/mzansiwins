Are Online Casino Games Legal in South Africa? 2026 Guide

The answer depends on the platform. Licensed bookmakers can offer casino-style games. Standalone offshore casinos cannot legally operate in South Africa. Here's the full breakdown.

By Thabo Mokoena  |  Updated April 2026  |  18+ only

South African gambling law draws a distinction between licensed online betting and interactive online gambling. Public guidance from the National Gambling Act framework and the NGB is consistent on the core point: online betting is lawful when offered by a bookmaker licensed by a South African provincial board, while interactive gambling remains restricted unless specifically authorised by national law. This page therefore focuses on locally licensed bookmakers that also make casino-style products available on their platforms. It does not treat offshore online casinos as equivalent alternatives.

What the National Gambling Act Says

The National Gambling Act 7 of 2004 and the National Gambling Amendment Act 10 of 2008 are the two pieces of legislation that govern gambling in South Africa. The Act established the National Gambling Board and empowered nine provincial gambling boards to issue licences for permitted forms of gambling within their territories.

The Act defines permitted gambling activities. Land-based casino licences exist and are issued by provincial boards to physical casinos. Online or "interactive" gambling is not included in the list of permitted activities under the Act unless specifically authorised. The 2008 amendment maintained this restriction, meaning no one has yet been granted a standalone interactive online casino licence in South Africa.

The relevant distinction for players is between:

  • Online sports betting and fixed-odds wagering - permitted through licensed bookmakers
  • Bookmaker-hosted casino-style products - available on some licensed bookmaker platforms via fixed-odds betting mechanisms
  • Standalone online casinos - not licensed under South African law

For a broader explanation of what is and isn't permitted under SA law, read the companion guide on whether online betting is legal in South Africa.

The NGB's Position on Interactive Gambling

The National Gambling Board's published guidance is direct: interactive gambling is prohibited except where conducted by operators licensed under the National Gambling Act framework, specifically licensed online bookmakers. The NGB points the public to its verified-operators portal as the definitive list of operators that are legally verified to accept bets from South African customers.

The NGB has also published enforcement guidance targeting unlicensed gambling sites operating without SA authorisation. Using platforms not listed on the verified-operators portal means operating outside the legal framework.

This matters practically. If you use a platform not on that list, you have no South African regulatory channel to escalate a complaint, no guarantee your deposits are protected, and no SA law governing how your personal data is handled.

Bookmaker-Hosted Casino Games vs. Offshore Casinos

Several SA-licensed bookmakers offer casino-style content, including slots, live dealer games, crash games, and table games. These are not standalone casino licences. The operator holds a provincial bookmaker licence and offers casino-style products as fixed-odds betting products within that licence framework. The distinction matters legally and practically.

The SCA Ruling on Fixed-Odds Bets and Casino Game Outcomes

A Supreme Court of Appeal ruling confirmed that licensed SA bookmakers can offer fixed-odds bets on the outcomes of casino-style games, including slots, under their bookmaker licence framework. This is the mechanism by which SA-licensed operators legally offer products like slots and live dealer games alongside their sports betting offering.

The ruling applies to licensed bookmakers operating within the SA regulatory framework. It does not make offshore casinos legal. An offshore platform cannot use this ruling to justify accepting SA customers without a provincial bookmaker licence.

The practical implication for players: if you want to play casino-style games within the legal framework, look for licensed bookmakers that offer this content under their SA provincial licences, not offshore sites.

How to Verify a Casino Platform's Licence

Before depositing on any platform that offers casino-style games, follow these steps:

  1. Check the platform's footer for a South African provincial bookmaker licence number
  2. Note the name of the licensing board (e.g. "Licensed by the Gauteng Gambling Board")
  3. Visit the NGB verified-operators portal and search for the operator name
  4. If the operator isn't listed, don't deposit

For a complete step-by-step guide, including how to use provincial board databases to cross-reference specific licence numbers, see the guide on how to verify a bookmaker licence in South Africa.

Player Protections on Licensed Platforms

When you use a licensed SA bookmaker for casino-style content, you benefit from regulatory protections that don't exist on offshore platforms:

  • FICA verification - Your identity is verified under SA financial intelligence law, which protects both you and the system from fraud
  • Segregated player funds - Your deposit balance is required to be held separately from the operator's working capital
  • Responsible gambling tools - Deposit limits, session timers, cooling-off periods, and self-exclusion are required by licence conditions
  • Consumer complaints - Unresolved disputes can be escalated to the provincial gambling board that issued the operator's licence
  • Fair gaming requirements - Licensed operators must comply with the terms of their provincial licence, which includes requirements around game fairness

All of these protections disappear the moment you move to an unlicensed offshore platform. That's the practical argument for staying within the licensed ecosystem, whatever games you want to play.

Set a deposit limit before you start. All major SA-licensed platforms offer this feature in their responsible gambling settings.

Responsible Gambling

Casino-style games are designed to be engaging. That makes it easy to spend more time and money than intended. Before you play, decide on a budget and a time limit, and use the deposit limit tools that every licensed SA bookmaker provides.

  • Set a deposit limit in your account settings before you start
  • Use the session time reminder feature if your platform offers it
  • Take a cooling-off break if you find yourself chasing losses
  • Use self-exclusion if playing stops being fun

South Africa's 24-hour responsible gambling helpline is 0800 006 008. It's free and confidential. The National Responsible Gambling Programme also offers online self-exclusion registration and referrals to counsellors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are online casino games legal in South Africa?
It depends on the platform. Standalone online casinos are not licensed under SA law. However, licensed bookmakers can offer casino-style products - slots, live dealer games, crash games - under their provincial bookmaker licences. Those are what MzansiWins covers on its casino sites page.
What does the National Gambling Act say about online casinos?
The National Gambling Act 7 of 2004 and the 2008 amendment restrict interactive gambling unless specifically authorised by national law. No standalone online casino licence has been issued in South Africa. Licensed bookmakers can offer casino-style betting products under their bookmaker licences.
What is the NGB's position on interactive online gambling?
The NGB's published guidance states that interactive gambling is prohibited except online betting through SA licensed bookmakers. The NGB's verified-operators portal is the recommended resource for checking whether an operator is legally verified.
What is the difference between a bookmaker-hosted casino game and an offshore casino?
A bookmaker-hosted casino game is offered by an operator with a valid SA provincial bookmaker licence, subject to SA regulatory oversight and consumer protections. An offshore casino holds a foreign licence only and operates entirely outside the SA regulatory framework.
What was the SCA ruling on fixed-odds bets and casino game outcomes?
The Supreme Court of Appeal ruled that fixed-odds bets placed on the outcomes of casino-style games can be offered by licensed bookmakers under their fixed-odds betting framework. This is the mechanism that permits SA-licensed operators to offer slots and similar games. It does not apply to offshore casinos.
How do I verify that a casino-style platform is locally licensed?
Check the platform's footer for a South African provincial bookmaker licence number and the name of the licensing board. Then cross-reference it on the NGB verified-operators portal. If the operator isn't listed, it isn't verified.
What player protections apply on licensed platforms?
Licensed SA bookmakers offering casino-style products must comply with FICA identity verification, responsible gambling tool requirements, segregated player funds, and consumer complaint procedures enforced by the licensing provincial board. These protections are absent on offshore platforms.
Where can I get help if gambling becomes a problem?
South Africa's 24-hour responsible gambling helpline is 0800 006 008. It is free and confidential. Set a deposit limit before you start. The National Responsible Gambling Programme at responsiblegambling.org.za also offers self-exclusion registration online.
