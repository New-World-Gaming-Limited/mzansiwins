Not all casino apps perform equally. These are the areas we check on every app we review.

RTP and Game Fairness

Return to Player (RTP) is the percentage of stakes a slot pays back over millions of spins. A slot with 96% RTP returns R96 for every R100 wagered theoretically. In SA, the National Gambling Act requires licensed games to meet minimum RTP thresholds. Look for operators that publish RTP figures in-app or on their websites. The best apps from providers like Pragmatic Play publish per-game RTP data you can verify.

Live casino on mobile depends on your data connection. On LTE or Wi-Fi, Evolution Gaming tables stream at 720p with minimal buffering. On 3G, some apps drop to lower quality streams. FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act) compliance requires all licensed SA betting operators to verify your identity before you can withdraw. Most apps handle this in-app: take a photo of your green barcoded ID or smart ID card, then a selfie and proof of address (utility bill or bank statement). Native App vs PWA vs Mobisite

A native app is downloaded and installed on your device. It can use push notifications, your camera for FICA, and tends to perform better with smoother animations. Examples: Betway (iOS), 10Bet (Android), Supersportbet (all platforms).

A PWA (Progressive Web App) is installed from your browser and sits on your home screen like a native app. It doesn't require enabling unknown sources, but lacks some native features like biometric login. Lulabet uses this approach on Android.

A mobisite is simply the operator's website optimised for mobile browsers. No installation is needed. Hollywoodbets uses this for Android. It's convenient but doesn't support push notifications and requires an internet connection to load each time.

Are Casino Apps Legal in South Africa?

A common question, with a straightforward answer.

Online gambling is legal in South Africa when conducted through a provincially licensed operator. The regulatory framework comes from the National Gambling Act 7 of 2004 and its amendments. Under this act, each province issues its own gambling licences. You'll see operators licensed in Gauteng (by the Gauteng Gambling Board), the Western Cape (WCGRB), KwaZulu-Natal (KZN Gambling Board), the Eastern Cape, and other provinces.

The key distinction is between sports betting apps with casino sections and pure-play casino apps. Most operators in this list are primarily sports betting operators that hold licences allowing them to also offer casino games. Jackpot City is an example of a casino-focused operator. Both types are legal, provided they hold valid SA licences.

Offshore casinos (operators without SA provincial licences) operate in a grey area. The National Gambling Act makes it an offence to conduct online gambling without a licence, but enforcement is directed at operators rather than players. That said, using an unlicensed operator means you have no consumer protection if a payment dispute arises. All operators in this list are fully licensed.

How to check a licence: Every licensed SA operator is required to display their licence details on their website and app. Look for the gambling board name and licence number in the footer. You can verify licence status directly with the relevant provincial gambling board.
