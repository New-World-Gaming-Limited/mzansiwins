What Is Live Casino?

Live casino is a format where a trained human dealer runs the game from a purpose-built studio while a camera feeds the action directly to your screen. You watch the dealer deal cards or spin the roulette wheel in real time, and you place your bets through the on-screen interface. It's different from standard RNG (Random Number Generator) casino games, where outcomes are determined by software, because here a physical wheel or deck of cards decides the result.

The experience is closer to sitting at a casino floor table than anything a standard digital slot can offer. You can often see other players' bets, chat with the dealer, and follow the game history displayed on screen. Popular live casino games include roulette, blackjack, baccarat, and poker variants. Game shows like Crazy Time and Lightning Roulette have added an entertainment layer that's attracted players who wouldn't traditionally play table games.

In South Africa, live casino is available at licensed operators only. Unlicensed offshore sites are not regulated under SA law, so always confirm that the operator holds a valid provincial gambling licence before depositing.

The most played live casino game in SA. Standard European Roulette has a house edge of 2.7%. Live Blackjack

Live blackjack gives SA players the best theoretical RTP of standard live table games, typically 99.5%+ with basic strategy. Live Baccarat

Baccarat has grown in popularity among SA live casino players. The game has simple rules: you bet on the Banker or Player hand. Banker bets carry the best odds with a house edge under 1.1%. Speed Baccarat from Evolution plays a hand roughly every 25 seconds, making it one of the fastest live table formats.

What is live casino?

Live casino is a format where real human dealers run games from a studio, streamed to your screen in real time. You place bets through the game interface and the outcome is determined by a physical wheel, cards, or game show mechanics rather than software.
