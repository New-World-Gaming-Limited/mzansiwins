By Sipho Nkosi  |  Updated April 2026  |  18+ only

The Currie Cup is South African rugby's domestic heartbeat. Since 1889, the provinces have competed for the same trophy, making it the oldest provincial rugby competition in the world. The 2026 Premier Division runs from 18 July to the final on 12 September, with Griquas defending the title they won in 2025. Eight teams contest the Premier Division: Bulls, Sharks, Western Province, and Lions are confirmed, with four more qualifying through the SA Cup, whose final takes place on 23 May 2026.

For bettors, the Currie Cup sits at a sweet spot: the matches are on free-to-air and subscription TV across South Africa, the rosters are deep enough to analyse, and most SA bookmakers cover every game in the draw. This guide covers the teams, the format, the betting markets, and where to place your bets.

Teams

Four franchises are confirmed for the 2026 Premier Division. These are South Africa's biggest rugby provinces, all with Springbok pipelines and large supporter bases. Four more teams will join them after the SA Cup final on 23 May 2026.

Bulls
Loftus Versfeld, Pretoria

The Bulls are the competition's most successful team in the modern era, winning the Premier Division in 2021, 2022, and 2023 before Griquas ended that run in 2025. They play out of Loftus Versfeld in Pretoria, one of the most difficult venues for visiting teams in domestic rugby. Their forward pack is typically the most powerful in the competition, and their game plan reflects that: direct, physical, and relentless at the breakdown. Any season opener at Loftus is a near-certain home win. On the road, particularly in Cape Town and Durban, they are more vulnerable and bettors can find value against them in away fixtures.

Sharks
Kings Park, Durban

The Sharks won the Currie Cup in 2024, beating the Bulls in the final. They are based at Kings Park in Durban, where the coastal conditions and humid climate can affect visiting teams from the highveld. The Sharks have produced a string of Springboks in recent seasons, and their backline has the pace to punish teams that leave gaps in defence. They are one of the title favourites for 2026. In betting terms, their home record at Kings Park is strong, and total points markets can be interesting in Durban where games tend to be higher-scoring than highveld encounters.

Western Province
DHL Stadium, Cape Town

Western Province (WP) are the competition's most-capped team historically, with more Currie Cup titles than any other province. They now operate as WP under the Stormers franchise umbrella. Cape Town is their home, and DHL Stadium is one of the best venues in the country. WP have struggled to match the consistency of the Bulls and Sharks in recent editions, but their squad remains packed with talent. They are a credible dark horse for 2026, especially given their home advantage in the Cape, where the weather can make high-altitude highveld teams uncomfortable.

Lions
Ellis Park, Johannesburg

The Lions play out of Ellis Park in Johannesburg. They have found it harder to compete with the Bulls for highveld supremacy in recent years, but their young squad has shown improvement and the city rivalry with the Bulls makes this fixture one of the highlights of the Currie Cup calendar. The Lions tend to play expansive rugby, which makes them unpredictable: capable of beating any team in the draw on their day, but also capable of conceding big scores when their defence is disorganised. Handicap markets involving the Lions can offer value, particularly in fixtures where they are large underdogs at home.

The four SA Cup qualifiers will be confirmed after 23 May 2026. Historically, teams like Griquas, Pumas, Cheetahs, and Border Bulldogs have contested the SA Cup places. Griquas, as defending Currie Cup champions, will be among the most closely watched of the SA Cup qualifiers. Once confirmed, this section will be updated with profiles for all eight teams.

Currie Cup First Division

Running alongside the SA Cup and ahead of the Premier Division, the Currie Cup First Division is the second tier of South African provincial rugby. Six teams compete in a round-robin format. The 2026 First Division opens on 13 June and the final is on 25 July.

Griffons are the defending First Division champions. The First Division features teams that did not qualify for the Premier Division and serves as both a competition in its own right and a pathway back into the top tier via the SA Cup. Teams in the First Division tend to have smaller budgets and less Springbok representation, but the games are competitive and outcomes are harder to predict, which can appeal to bettors looking for less-efficient markets. Most SA bookmakers cover First Division matches with match result and handicap markets.

How to Bet on the Currie Cup

Most SA bookmakers cover every Premier Division match with a range of markets. Match result is the entry point, but serious rugby bettors tend to spend more time on handicap and totals. Here's how each market works.

Match Result Low variance

The simplest bet: pick the team that wins. In the Currie Cup, home advantage is significant. The Bulls at Loftus, Sharks at Kings Park, and WP at DHL Stadium all carry a clear edge. On neutral or away ground, the margin narrows. Match result is best used when you have a strong read on the home team's form and the visitor's travel schedule.

Markets: Home/Away (no draw in knock-outs)
Handicap Betting Medium variance

Bookmakers set a points spread, typically between 5 and 15 points in most Currie Cup fixtures. You're betting on whether the favourite wins by more than the spread, or the underdog loses by less. Handicap markets are often better value than match result because the margins in South African provincial rugby are regularly tight, and strong teams don't always cover large spreads when playing away.

Tip: Away handicaps are often mispriced early in the season
Try Scorers High variance

First try scorer and anytime try scorer are popular Currie Cup markets. Wingers and outside backs are the most frequent scorers in open play, while hookers score more often from lineout drives at close range. Anytime try scorer at longer odds (10/1 or more) on a prolific winger across a full season can build into meaningful returns. Most bookmakers offer both first scorer and anytime scorer for Premier Division matches.

Best for: Accumulators across multiple games
Tournament Winner High variance

Outright winner markets open once the full eight-team draw is confirmed. The Bulls, Sharks, and Griquas (as defenders) are typically priced as co-favourites. These markets are best placed early, before mid-season form narrows the odds on the leaders. A pre-season investment on a team at long odds, such as Western Province at a title price, can generate significant returns if they make the final. Check the best rugby betting sites for the widest range of outright prices.

Best timing: Pre-season or Round 1
Top Points Scorer Medium variance

Season-long market on the player who finishes with the most points from penalties, conversions, and tries. Flyhalves who regularly kick for goal and play every match dominate this market. If a team's first-choice flyhalf has a clear run through the season without injury, they're the target. Check injury news in the first two rounds before committing. Only a small number of SA bookmakers offer this market, so availability varies.

Key factor: Playing time and kicking duty

A note on totals: total points over/under is available on most Currie Cup matches. High-altitude venues (Loftus at 1,354m, Ellis Park at 1,753m above sea level) tend to produce slightly higher-scoring games because the ball carries further in thinner air. Coastal venues like Kings Park in Durban, particularly in humid conditions, can be lower-scoring. Keep this in mind when assessing over/under lines.

History

The Currie Cup has been contested since 1889, making it the oldest provincial rugby union competition in the world. The trophy was donated by Sir Donald Currie, a shipping magnate, who gifted it to the team that performed best against the first touring British rugby side to visit South Africa. Griqualand West, the team that awarded the cup after the tour, chose to present it as a prize for provincial competition rather than keep it, and South African rugby has contested it ever since.

Western Province dominated the competition for most of its history, winning more titles than any other province. In the modern professional era, the Bulls became the dominant force, particularly between 2009 and 2023. Recent seasons have seen the title move between teams, reflecting the closer competitive balance that the SA Cup qualification system brings.

The Sharks were finalists in all three of those years, which illustrates how the competition has become a two-province contest in recent cycles. Griquas' 2025 win was a significant upset and ended that pattern, mirroring their surprise title in 2020 when they became the first smaller province to win in the professional era.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the Currie Cup 2026 Premier Division start?

The opening round of the 2026 Currie Cup Premier Division is on 18 July 2026. The final is scheduled for 12 September 2026. The competition runs over approximately eight weeks of round-robin play, followed by semi-finals and the final.

Who are the defending Currie Cup champions?

Griquas are the defending Currie Cup Premier Division champions, having won the title in 2025. They will need to qualify through the SA Cup for 2026 unless the qualification rules place them directly into the Premier Division as defenders.

How many teams are in the 2026 Currie Cup Premier Division?

Eight teams will contest the 2026 Premier Division. Bulls, Sharks, Western Province, and Lions are confirmed. The remaining four spots are filled by teams who qualify through the SA Cup, with the SA Cup final taking place on 23 May 2026.

What is the Currie Cup First Division?

The Currie Cup First Division is the second tier of South African provincial rugby. Six teams compete in 2026, with the competition running from 13 June to the final on 25 July. Griffons are the defending First Division champions.

What bet types are available for Currie Cup betting?

All major SA bookmakers offer match result, handicap, and total points over/under for Currie Cup Premier Division matches. Try scorer markets (first scorer and anytime scorer) are available at most. Tournament winner outrights and top points scorer for the season are available at select bookmakers including Hollywoodbets, Betway, and World Sports Betting.

Is the Currie Cup the oldest rugby competition in the world?

The Currie Cup is widely regarded as the oldest provincial rugby union competition in the world. It has been contested since 1889, predating most other domestic rugby competitions globally. The trophy was donated by Sir Donald Currie after the first British tour to South Africa.
