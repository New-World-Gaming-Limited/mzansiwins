Betting in the Western Cape: Overview

The Western Cape is home to the most active provincial gambling regulator in South Africa. The WCGRB has issued licences to over 20 online bookmakers, more than any other provincial board. This makes the Western Cape the operational hub for much of SA's licensed online betting industry.

The province has a strong sporting culture built around the Stormers in rugby, Cape Town City and Stellenbosch FC in football, the Cape Cobras in cricket, and horse racing at Kenilworth. All of these are well-covered by the bookmakers listed on this page.

The Western Cape government has committed R25 million toward gambling regulation infrastructure, signalling a continued investment in responsible and well-regulated online betting in the province.

The WCGRB and Western Cape Gambling Regulation

The Western Cape Gambling and Racing Board (WCGRB) operates under the Western Cape Gambling and Racing Act, No. 4 of 1996. It is the licensing and enforcement authority for all gambling in the Western Cape, covering online sports betting, casinos, horse racing totalisators, bingo halls, and limited payout machines.

Any bookmaker holding a WCGRB licence must comply with the board's conditions, which include responsible gambling measures, age verification systems, and regular audits. The WCGRB publishes its licensed operator list on wcgrb.co.za, and you can verify any operator's licence status there.

Cape Town residents can self-exclude from gambling at WCGRB-licensed operators through the board's exclusion register. This covers all licensed platforms and is a practical tool for anyone who wants to take a break from betting.

Popular Sports for Western Cape Bettors

Rugby: The Stormers

Rugby is the top spectator sport in the Western Cape. The Stormers play at DHL Newlands (Cape Town Stadium for big matches) and compete in the United Rugby Championship. They drew over 50,000 fans when they won the URC in 2022. During the URC season, all major SA bookmakers offer full Stormers coverage including match result, handicap, and first try scorer markets.

Football: Cape Town City and Stellenbosch FC

The Western Cape has two PSL sides. Cape Town City plays at the Cape Town Stadium and has a strong following. Stellenbosch FC is based in the Winelands and plays at Danie Craven Stadium. Both clubs are covered in PSL betting markets at all 44 operators on this page.

Cricket: Cape Cobras and Newlands

Newlands is one of the world's most scenic cricket grounds and has hosted Ashes matches, Test series, and SA20 fixtures. The Cape Cobras represent the Western Cape in franchise cricket. Cricket betting markets are available at most operators, with Betway and 10Bet having particularly strong IPL and SA20 coverage.

Horse Racing: Kenilworth

Kenilworth racecourse hosts the J&B Met, Cape's biggest horse racing event. The racecourse also holds the L'Ormarins Queen's Plate and other premier flat race meetings. Hollywoodbets, World Sports Betting, and betXchange offer the deepest racing markets and live streaming for Western Cape meetings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Western Cape Gambling and Racing Board?

The Western Cape Gambling and Racing Board (WCGRB) is the provincial gambling regulator for the Western Cape. It licences bookmakers, casinos, horse racing totalisators, and limited payout machines in the province. The WCGRB is widely regarded as the most active provincial gambling board in South Africa and has issued licences to the largest number of online bookmakers.

How much does the Western Cape invest in gambling regulation?

The Western Cape government has committed R25 million toward upgrading gambling regulation infrastructure and responsible gambling programmes. This investment is part of a broader strategy to strengthen oversight of the rapidly growing online betting sector, including improved compliance monitoring and problem gambling support services.

Which bookmakers are WCGRB-licensed?

A large number of SA bookmakers hold WCGRB licences, including Easybet, Betway, World Sports Betting, Pantherbet, Topbet, Jabula Bets, Supersportbet, Betshezi, betXchange, YesPlay, Lulabet, Bet.co.za, Sunbet, Swifty, Apexbets, Betjets, PlayTsogo, BetOlimp, NEO.bet, PalaceBet, and Sportpesa. The full WCGRB licence list is on the board's official website at wcgrb.co.za.

Can I bet on the Stormers from Cape Town?

Yes. All 44 operators listed here offer URC and Super Rugby markets where the Stormers are available. Betway, Supersportbet, World Sports Betting, and Hollywoodbets tend to have the deepest URC coverage. During the Stormers' championship runs, most operators offer enhanced pre-match odds and special betting markets.

Is horse racing betting popular in the Western Cape?

Yes. Kenilworth racecourse in Cape Town hosts regular meetings including prestigious flat race events. The J&B Met is the most popular racing event in the Western Cape and attracts significant betting volumes. World Sports Betting, Hollywoodbets, and betXchange have strong horse racing coverage with live streaming of meetings.

What payment methods work in Cape Town?

All major South African banks work for EFT deposits. Capitec Pay and Ozow are the fastest deposit options. OTT Vouchers are widely available at Cape Town retailers. Most operators also accept Visa and Mastercard. Withdrawals arrive within 1 to 3 business days via EFT.
