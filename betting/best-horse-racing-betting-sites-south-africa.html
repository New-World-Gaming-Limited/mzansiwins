Horse Racing Betting Types

Horse racing has the widest variety of bet types of any sport in South Africa. Understanding each type helps you pick the right market for your budget and risk tolerance.

  • Win Simplest bet. Your horse must finish first. Best payout but lowest probability.
  • Place Your horse must finish in the top two or three, depending on the field size. Lower odds, better chance of winning.
  • Swinger Pick two horses to finish first and second in any order. Easier than an Exacta.
  • Exacta Name the first and second horse in the correct order. Higher risk, higher reward than a Swinger.
  • Trifecta Predict the first, second, and third horse in the exact finishing order. Can return very large payouts when outsiders fill the places.
  • Pick 6 Select the winners of six consecutive races. Pools carry over when there's no winner.
  • Jackpot Similar to Pick 6 but may cover fewer races and have different pool rules, check each operator's specific terms.
  • Place Accumulator Link multiple horse races with each selection needing to place (not necessarily win). Balances risk across several events.

Start with Win or Place bets if you're new to horse racing. Exactas and Trifectas require reading the full form closely and understanding how the draw, track bias, and going affect your selections.

Tote vs Fixed Odds

The two main ways to bet on horse racing in South Africa are Tote (pari-mutuel) pools and fixed odds.

Tote Pools

With Tote betting, all stakes go into a pool and the operator takes a percentage. The remaining pool is divided among winning tickets. Your payout depends on how many people backed the same horse. A popular winner means small returns; an outsider means big returns. Hollywoodbets and World Sports Betting run full Tote integration.

Fixed Odds

Fixed odds lock in your return at the moment you click confirm. You know exactly what you'll receive if your horse wins. Odds are usually set by the bookmaker and move up or down with betting activity. Betway and Sportingbet specialise in fixed-odds racing.

On major meetings like the Durban July, both options are often available side by side. Tote pools tend to offer better value on outsiders; fixed odds work better when you back a short-priced favourite before it shortens further.

Major SA Racing Events

South Africa's racing calendar has several flagship meetings that attract national attention and peak betting volumes.

Hollywoodbets Durban July

Run at Greyville Racecourse each July, the Durban July is the country's most celebrated horse race. The fashion festival and social calendar around the race makes it an annual national occasion. Bookmakers compete aggressively on prices for the big day.

Cape Town Met (L'Ormarins Queen's Plate)

Held at Kenilworth Racecourse in Cape Town each January, the Met is the second-biggest race on the SA calendar. It attracts strong Cape-bred horses and deep international interest.

Summer Cup

Hosted at Turffontein in Johannesburg each November, the Summer Cup is a 2,000m Grade 1 event that often attracts horses preparing for the Durban July the following year.

SA Derby

The SA Derby, run at Turffontein, is restricted to three-year-olds and is the most prestigious staying race for that age group. Classic form horses tend to be well-supported by punters.

Horse Racing Tips

Form Guides

Form guides show each horse's recent race results, the track conditions, distances, jockeys, and weight carried. Most SA bookmakers link to form guides or display recent runs. Take 10 minutes to read the form before placing a bet.

Trainer and Jockey Stats

Some trainer-jockey combinations produce consistently strong results at specific tracks. Hollywoodbets and World Sports Betting display win percentages for trainers and jockeys. A high-strike-rate combination at a meeting you're betting on is worth noting.

Track Conditions

Track conditions, firm, good, soft, or heavy, affect different horses differently. Some horses are bred to handle deep going; others prefer firm ground. Check the going report on race morning before finalising your selections.

Live Racing and Streaming

Hollywoodbets streams race meetings from Greyville, Kenilworth, Turffontein, and Scottsville on their website and app. Betway offers streaming on selected meetings. Both require you to be logged in with a funded account to access streams.

Streaming allows you to watch the parade ring, assess how horses are moving, and make more informed late in-play bets on running positions and pace. This is particularly valuable in longer races where the early tempo determines the outcome.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which SA bookmakers offer Tote betting on horse racing?

Hollywoodbets and World Sports Betting offer full Tote pool integration. Betway offers fixed-odds racing with selected tote pools on major meetings.

What is the Pick 6 bet?

The Pick 6 requires you to correctly select the winners of six consecutive races on a card. The pool carries over when there's no winner, creating large jackpots on popular racing days.

What is a Swinger bet?

A Swinger bet requires you to pick two horses that will finish first and second in any order. It's easier than an Exacta and pays lower odds, making it a popular starting point for exotic betting.

What is the Hollywoodbets Durban July?

The Hollywoodbets Durban July, run each July at Greyville Racecourse in Durban, is South Africa's most prestigious horse race. It draws the biggest betting volumes of any SA racing event and attracts wide media coverage.

What is fixed odds versus tote betting?

Fixed odds lock in your return at the time you place the bet. Tote betting means your return depends on how the pool is divided among all winning tickets, you don't know the final payout until the race closes.

Which tracks are covered by SA bookmakers?

Major SA tracks include Greyville (Durban), Kenilworth (Cape Town), Turffontein (Johannesburg), and Scottsville (Pietermaritzburg). Most licensed bookmakers cover all four.

Can I watch races live online at SA bookmakers?

Hollywoodbets and Betway offer live streaming on selected race meetings. Check the live streaming section on each site before race day to confirm coverage.

Is horse racing betting legal in South Africa?

Yes, at operators licensed by a provincial gambling board. All sites listed on MzansiWins hold valid SA gambling licences and are authorised to accept horse racing bets.
