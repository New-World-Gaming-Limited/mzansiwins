How Free Bets Work

A free bet is a betting credit, not real money. The operator allocates a fixed amount to your bonus wallet. You pick a market, choose your stake (up to the free bet amount), and place the wager. If it wins, you receive the profit only. The original stake is not returned. That distinction matters: a R50 free bet at odds of 3.00 returns R100 in profit, not R150 total.

There are three main types of free bet offer in SA.

No-Deposit Free Bets

These are credited on registration, before you put any money in. Easybet and Supabets both offer R50 free this way. You don't need a card to claim them - just complete the registration, verify your phone number, and the credit appears in your account. The value is limited but the risk is zero.

Matched Free Bets

Place a qualifying bet and the bookmaker matches your stake with a free bet of the same value. Mzansibet and Lulabet both use this model (bet R50, get R50). The qualifying bet has to meet minimum odds conditions, typically 1.50 or higher.

Risk-Free First Bets

If your qualifying bet loses, the bookmaker refunds your stake as a free bet or bonus cash. You're not truly risk-free since you're spending your own money, but you get a second attempt. These tend to have tighter minimum odds requirements to prevent value arbitrage.

Free Bet Terms Explained

Free bet offers come with conditions. Understanding them before you claim is the difference between enjoying the offer and being frustrated by it.

Wagering Requirements

A wagering requirement (sometimes called rollover or playthrough) specifies how many times you need to wager your winnings before they can be withdrawn. A 3x wagering requirement on a R50 free bet win of R100 means you'd need to bet a total of R300 before cashing out. Lower is better. Some no-deposit free bets come with 1x wagering.

Minimum Odds

Most offers require the free bet to be placed on a market at minimum odds, usually between 1.50 and 2.00. A match at 1.20 odds doesn't qualify. This stops people placing free bets on near-certainty outcomes and profiting without real betting activity.

Expiry Period

Unused free bets expire. Most SA offers give you between 7 and 30 days. If you don't use the credit before the deadline, it's gone. Set a calendar reminder when you claim, especially if you're claiming multiple offers across different bookmakers.

Maximum Winnings

Some operators cap the amount you can win from a free bet, typically between R500 and R5,000. That's not an issue on a R50 free bet at standard odds, but on a long-shot multi-bet, the cap can limit your return. Check whether a maximum applies before placing.

Tips for Using Free Bets

  • ✓Use free bets on singles, not complicated multi-bets. The more legs you add, the harder it is to win. A well-researched single on a match you know is often better value than a long-shot 10-leg acca.
  • ✓Check the minimum odds before building your selection. Using a free bet on a 1.10 favourite, only to discover it doesn't qualify, wastes the offer.
  • ✓Don't use free bets to chase losses. A free bet is an opportunity, not a recovery tool. If you've had a bad run with your own money, gambling more in any form isn't the answer.

Free Bets FAQ

What is a free bet in South Africa?

A free bet is a credit given by a bookmaker that lets you place a wager without risking your own money. If the bet wins, you receive the profit but not the stake. Most SA free bet offers are worth between R10 and R100.

Do I need to deposit to get a free bet?

Not always. Easybet and Supabets both offer R50 free on signup without requiring a deposit first. Other offers require a qualifying deposit before the free bet is credited.

Can I withdraw a free bet?

You can't withdraw the free bet token itself. When the free bet wins, you receive the winnings (profit portion) as real cash, which is withdrawable after meeting any minimum odds or wagering conditions.

What are wagering requirements on free bets?

Wagering requirements specify how many times you need to use the winnings before withdrawing. A 3x requirement on a free bet win of R100 means you need to place R300 in total bets before cashing out. Always check the T&Cs before claiming.

How long do free bets last?

Most SA free bets expire within 7 to 30 days of being credited. Set a reminder when you claim so you don't let it expire unused.

What is a risk-free bet?

A risk-free bet means if your first qualifying wager loses, the bookmaker refunds the stake as bonus cash or a free bet. You're still spending money - but you get a second chance with equivalent credit if the bet loses.

Can I claim a free bet at multiple bookmakers?

Yes. Each offer is claimed once per person per bookmaker. Holding accounts at several sites is common and lets you access the best odds and multiple welcome offers.

Are free bets available to existing customers?

Yes. Several SA bookmakers offer reload bonuses, enhanced odds, and acca insurance for existing players. These are usually in the promotions tab of your account. Hollywoodbets and Betway both run ongoing promotions for active players.

Are SA free bet offers legitimate?

Offers from SA-licensed bookmakers are legitimate. All operators on this page hold provincial gambling licences under the National Gambling Act.

Do I need to be FICA verified to claim a free bet?

You don't always need to be FICA verified to claim a free bet, but you will need to complete FICA verification before withdrawing winnings. Our FICA guide explains what's required.
