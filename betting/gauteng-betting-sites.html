Betting in Gauteng: What You Need to Know

Gauteng is South Africa's economic hub and home to the country's highest concentration of sports fans. The province holds two of the country's most-watched football clubs in Kaizer Chiefs and Orlando Pirates, plus SuperSport Park in Centurion and two Super Rugby franchises in the Lions and the Bulls. This makes Gauteng one of the most active sports betting provinces in the country.

Every operator listed below holds a valid South African provincial gambling licence and accepts bets from Gauteng residents. Three operators hold licences specifically issued by the Gauteng Gambling Board: World Sports Betting, betXchange, and Playbet.co.za. The rest operate under licences from other provincial boards that are valid nationally under the National Gambling Act.

Payment options common in Gauteng include FNB (the bank with the largest retail footprint in the province), Capitec, Nedbank, and Absa. Instant EFT via Ozow works at most platforms and settles within seconds, which is useful for quick top-ups before a match.

The Gauteng Gambling Board (GGB)

The Gauteng Gambling Board is the provincial regulator established under the Gauteng Gambling Act, No. 4 of 1995. It licenses and monitors bookmakers, casinos, bingo halls, and limited payout machines within the province. Any bookmaker wanting to operate from Gauteng or specifically target Gauteng customers must hold a GGB sports betting licence.

In practice, most SA bookmakers operate nationally under licences from one of the nine provincial boards. The National Gambling Act allows a nationally licenced operator to take bets from any province. So even if a bookmaker holds a Western Cape or Mpumalanga licence, it can legally accept bets from Johannesburg and Pretoria residents.

The GGB actively enforces responsible gambling rules, including self-exclusion registers, age verification requirements, and advertising restrictions. If you want to self-exclude from gambling platforms in Gauteng, contact the GGB directly or use the National Self-Exclusion database.

Popular Sports for Gauteng Bettors

Football (PSL)

The PSL generates the largest betting volume of any sport in Gauteng. Kaizer Chiefs and Orlando Pirates are two of the biggest clubs in Africa, and their rivalry produces the Soweto Derby, one of the most-watched matches on the continent. Mamelodi Sundowns, based in Pretoria, have been the dominant force in SA football in recent seasons and consistently attract strong betting markets.

Rugby

Gauteng has two professional rugby franchises: the Lions (Ellis Park) and the Bulls (Loftus Versfeld). Both play in the United Rugby Championship (URC) and compete in European competition. Ellis Park derbies between the Lions and Bulls attract strong betting interest. The Springboks also draw significant betting volumes during Test windows.

Cricket

SuperSport Park in Centurion is one of South Africa's premier Test and ODI venues. Gauteng-based fans follow the Titans in franchise cricket. SA20 has added a new layer of cricket betting, with the Pretoria Capitals attracting new bettors to cricket markets.

Horse Racing

Turffontein racecourse in Johannesburg and Greyville in KZN are the main tracks. The Computaform Sprint and J&B Met are the biggest flat races. Most bookmakers offer tote betting and fixed-odds racing markets. Hollywoodbets and World Sports Betting have the deepest horse racing coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Gauteng Gambling Board license online betting sites?

Yes. The Gauteng Gambling Board (GGB) issues sports betting licences to operators who want to take bets from Gauteng residents. Operators like World Sports Betting and betXchange hold GGB licences. Most major SA bookmakers hold licences from at least one provincial board and accept bets from Gauteng players legally.

Can I bet online from Johannesburg and Pretoria?

Yes. Online betting is legal for adults 18 and over across Gauteng. Residents of Johannesburg, Pretoria, Ekurhuleni, and other Gauteng metros can register with any licensed SA bookmaker and place bets online or via a mobile app.

Which payment methods work best for Gauteng bettors?

FNB, Capitec, and Nedbank are the most widely used banks in Gauteng and are accepted at almost every SA bookmaker. Ozow and Peach Payments offer instant EFT and are popular for fast deposits. Standard Bank and Absa customers can also use EFT at most sites.

What sports can I bet on from Gauteng?

Football (PSL) is the most bet-on sport, followed by rugby (Lions and Bulls are Gauteng teams), cricket, horse racing, and tennis. Bookmakers also offer markets for international football, the URC, and Super Rugby. All 44 operators listed here cover PSL matches.

Are Kaizer Chiefs and Orlando Pirates popular for betting?

Very. The Soweto Derby between Chiefs and Pirates attracts some of the highest betting volumes of any SA football match. Most bookmakers offer enhanced odds promotions for this fixture. Sundowns, now the country's top club, also generates strong betting interest across all platforms.

What is the Gauteng Gambling Act?

The Gauteng Gambling Act, No. 4 of 1995 as amended, governs gambling in Gauteng province. It establishes the Gauteng Gambling Board as the licensing and enforcement authority. Online sports betting operators must hold a valid licence under the National Gambling Act and, where operating from Gauteng, comply with provincial regulations too.
