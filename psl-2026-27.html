Season Overview

The DStv Premiership is the top division of South African football, sitting at the peak of the South African football pyramid. Sixteen clubs compete across 30 matchdays in a round-robin format, with each team playing every other club home and away over a season that typically runs from mid-August to May.

Mamelodi Sundowns are the dominant force in the current era. They have won multiple consecutive league titles and are a regular presence in the CAF Champions League, the continent's premier club competition. Sundowns' depth of squad, coaching quality, and financial resources set them apart from the rest of the division, though the gap to the chasing pack has been narrowing.

The relegation picture is always tense. Two clubs drop to the GladAfrica Championship at the end of each season, while promotion play-offs determine who comes up. The fight to avoid the bottom two creates some of the most volatile betting opportunities in the division, particularly in the final third of the season.

Teams to Watch

Four clubs will attract the bulk of the betting action in 2026/27. Their contrasting situations make each an interesting proposition at different markets.

Mamelodi Sundowns
Title Favourites
Sundowns are the benchmark in South African club football. Based at Loftus Versfeld in Pretoria, they play with a large budget, a deep squad, and the kind of tactical organisation that makes them difficult to beat in a long-haul league campaign. Their consistent participation in the CAF Champions League brings exposure to continental-quality opponents, which sharpens the squad. Mamelodi Sundowns will be short-priced outright favourites from day one of the season.
Stadium: Loftus Versfeld, Pretoria Market: Title, CAF qualification
Kaizer Chiefs
Biggest Fanbase
No club in South Africa carries a following like Kaizer Chiefs. Based in Naturena, Soweto, they have historically been the most successful club in the country, though recent seasons have been difficult. Management brought in new coaching staff with a mandate to return to title contention. Chiefs' home matches consistently draw the largest crowds in the league, and any improved form translates quickly into competitive outright odds. They are the best-priced team with genuine title potential.
Stadium: Naturena, Soweto Market: Top 4, title each-way
Orlando Pirates
Strong Recent Form
Orlando Pirates have been the most consistent challenger to Sundowns in recent seasons. Playing out of Orlando Stadium, they bring an organised defensive structure and the ability to win ugly, which counts for a lot over a 30-game season. The Soweto rivalry with Kaizer Chiefs gives their season a narrative thread that keeps fans and bettors engaged regardless of league position. Pirates are capable of sustaining a title challenge, and they have the squad depth to manage CAF and domestic duties simultaneously.
Stadium: Orlando Stadium, Soweto Market: Title, top scorer
Stellenbosch FC
Emerging Force
Stellenbosch are the most interesting story in PSL football. Based in the Cape Winelands, they have consistently overperformed relative to their budget through shrewd coaching and a well-defined playing style. They have finished above clubs with significantly larger resources in each of the last few seasons, making their outright odds better value than they initially appear. If you are looking for a long-shot title bet, or a confident top-half finish bet, Stellenbosch are the team to assess first.
Stadium: Danie Craven Stadium, Stellenbosch Market: Top half, top 6

Other clubs to follow

Beyond the big four, several clubs will influence the title race and provide value in specific markets. AmaZulu (Durban) carry strong support in KwaZulu-Natal and tend to be competitive at home. Golden Arrows, also Durban-based, punch above their weight in streaky form that bettors can exploit in match-by-match markets. SuperSport United from Pretoria have the facilities and experience to be consistent challengers for cup competition. Cape Town City play an attractive style under consistent management, making them a decent bet for top-6 finishes.

PSL Betting Markets

South African bookmakers offer a wide range of markets on DStv Premiership matches. Below is a breakdown of the main market types, with notes on where value tends to appear in PSL football specifically.

Match Result (1X2) Core Market
Back a home win, draw, or away win. PSL football produces a relatively high draw rate compared to European leagues, partly because of the physical conditions and the tactical caution many mid-table clubs employ. Always check recent head-to-head records and home/away form separately, as some clubs are dramatically different propositions at home versus away.
Note: High draw rate Best for: Most fixtures
Correct Score High Risk
Predict the exact final score. PSL matches are often low-scoring, so 1-0, 0-0, and 1-1 are among the most frequent results. These outcomes are priced longer than in higher-scoring leagues, offering reasonable returns for a well-researched correct score bet in a match featuring two defensively solid sides.
Common scores: 1-0, 0-0, 1-1 Odds range: 6/1 to 30/1+
Over/Under Goals Popular
Bet on whether the total goals in a match exceed or fall below a set line, typically 2.5. PSL matches trend toward under 2.5 goals in most fixtures involving mid-table and lower-table clubs. Matches between the big clubs can go either way, with the Sundowns vs Chiefs fixture, for example, capable of both high-scoring and tight finishes depending on the stage of the season.
Key line: 2.5 goals Trend: Under-heavy
Both Teams to Score Medium Risk
Will both clubs score at least once? BTTS No is often good value in PSL matches between Sundowns and mid-table opponents, as Sundowns' defensive record is strong enough to keep clean sheets regularly. BTTS Yes becomes more interesting in Soweto Derby fixtures and matches where both sides are pressing for results late in the season.
Best angle: Lopsided fixtures Season impact: Late season shifts
First/Anytime Goalscorer Medium Risk
Back a specific player to score first or at any point in the match. Player prop markets in the PSL are not as deep as in European leagues, but the leading bookmakers carry both first scorer and anytime scorer for most high-profile matches. Top striker form, starting lineup news, and whether a player is on set-piece duty are the key inputs.
Key factor: Starting XI news Set pieces: Major driver
Outright Champion Season-Long
Back a team to win the DStv Premiership at the season's end. Sundowns will be short, so the value question is whether they shorten or drift during the season, and whether Kaizer Chiefs or Pirates hit a run of form that shifts market sentiment. Outright odds are typically available from pre-season, giving long-lead-time bettors the chance to lock in prices before they move.
Opens: Pre-season Value pick: Chiefs at any price above 5/1
Top Scorer Long-Shot
Bet on which player will finish as the Premiership's top goalscorer. This market often comes down to whether a Sundowns striker gets consistent game time, or whether a smaller club's lone striker accumulates goals in a season where his club battles relegation. The market is thin pre-season but worth watching as it updates once the leading strikers become clear around matchday 8 to 10.
Typical winner: 12-20 goals Pre-season odds: Wide range
Relegation Season-Long
Bet on which clubs will be relegated to the GladAfrica Championship. Two sides go down each season. Newly promoted clubs and clubs with weaker squads and form from the prior season are the starting point for analysis. Relegation odds move significantly mid-season as the standings clarify, so there are usually two distinct windows of value: pre-season and around matchday 15 to 20.
Relegated: Bottom 2 Key period: Mid-season drift
Handicap Betting Value Play
Asian handicap and European handicap markets level the playing field in mismatched fixtures. When Sundowns play a bottom-table side, a -1.5 or -2 handicap bet can offer similar odds to a straight result bet in a more competitive match. Handicap markets are worth exploring in fixtures involving the top two or three clubs facing significantly weaker opponents, where the match result market has been priced too tight on the stronger side to find value.
Best use: Lopsided fixtures Market type: Asian & European handicap Availability: Most top SA bookmakers

Soweto Derby Guide

Kaizer Chiefs versus Orlando Pirates is the most-watched club football fixture in South Africa, and it generates the highest single-match betting volumes of any PSL game. The rivalry dates to the early years of professional football in South Africa and carries a cultural weight that goes well beyond football. Both clubs draw support from across the country, not just from Soweto, and their matches regularly attract 80,000 or more spectators when held at FNB Stadium.

Brief history

The fixture has been contested hundreds of times across league, cup, and preseason formats. No single club has dominated the series to the point of sustained superiority, which is part of what makes each encounter so unpredictable. There have been seasons where one club wins both league derbies, and seasons where both end in stalemate. The psychological stakes are high enough that form heading into a derby can be a poor predictor of the result.

Betting angles

The draw is historically the most common single outcome in the Soweto Derby. Both teams tend to approach the fixture with caution, and the occasion itself creates pressure that disrupts free-flowing football. From a pure betting angle, the draw no bet market, or backing the draw at any price above evens, has been a consistent approach for bettors who have tracked the series over many seasons.

Over/Under 2.5 goals leans toward Under in derbies, though high-scoring exceptions do occur when one side is in superior form. BTTS No is worth considering when one club enters the game badly out of form and has scored in fewer than half their recent matches. Goalscorer markets are particularly well-priced because the intensity of the match suppresses goalscoring, so the odds on regular strikers hitting the net stay longer than their general form would suggest.

The fixture is held twice in the league season. The second derby, in the spring or early new year, often carries more league-table significance, which tends to produce more open football from the side that needs the points. That second match is usually the better bet on both teams to score and on over 2.5 goals.

Competitions

South African top-flight clubs compete across three main domestic competitions during the season. Each carries its own betting angle.

🏆
DStv Premiership
The 16-team league competition running from August to May. 30 matchdays per club. Two teams are relegated. The top two qualify for CAF competitions. Title betting, relegation betting, and top scorer markets all run for the full season.
⚽
Nedbank Cup
South Africa's knockout cup, open to clubs from all professional divisions. The equivalent of England's FA Cup, it gives lower-division clubs the chance to face Premiership opposition. Giant-killing results are common, which creates strong value in early rounds for any informed bettor willing to back a lower-division side on their home ground.
🥇
MTN8
A preseason knockout tournament restricted to the top 8 finishers from the prior Premiership season. It serves as the curtain-raiser to the South African football calendar, giving fans and bettors an early read on where each of the big clubs are in terms of form and squad development before the league season begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the PSL 2026/27 season start?

The DStv Premiership 2026/27 season is expected to kick off in mid-August 2026. The exact start date and fixture list will be confirmed by the Premier Soccer League closer to the season. Check this page for updates once fixtures are released.

Who are the defending PSL champions?

Mamelodi Sundowns are the defending DStv Premiership champions, having won multiple consecutive league titles. They enter the 2026/27 season as heavy favourites to retain the title.

How many teams play in the PSL?

The DStv Premiership has 16 teams playing in a round-robin format across 30 matchdays. Each team plays 30 matches over the course of the season, which runs from August to May.

Which bookmakers are best for PSL betting in South Africa?

Hollywoodbets leads for PSL coverage, largely because they sponsor multiple top-flight clubs and carry the widest range of PSL markets. Betway, World Sports Betting, Sportingbet, and Gbets are also strong options, all offering match result, correct score, goalscorer, and outright markets on the Premiership.

What is the Soweto Derby?

The Soweto Derby is the match between Kaizer Chiefs and Orlando Pirates. It is the biggest club football fixture in South Africa, drawing some of the largest crowds in African football. The two clubs share a deep rivalry that dates back decades, and the match consistently produces some of the highest betting volumes of the entire PSL season.

What competitions do PSL clubs compete in?

South African top-flight clubs compete in three main competitions: the DStv Premiership (the 16-team league), the Nedbank Cup (a knockout cup open to teams from all divisions), and the MTN8 (a preseason knockout tournament for the top 8 finishers from the previous Premiership season). Top clubs also participate in CAF continental competitions.

Can I bet on PSL matches online in South Africa?

Yes. All major South African licensed bookmakers accept PSL bets online and via mobile apps. You need a verified account with a deposited balance. Hollywoodbets, Betway, World Sports Betting, Sportingbet, and Gbets all offer PSL markets including match result, goalscorers, handicaps, over/under, and outright markets.

What PSL betting markets are available?

SA bookmakers typically offer match result (1X2), correct score, over/under goals, both teams to score, first goalscorer, anytime goalscorer, outright champion, top scorer, relegation, and Asian handicap markets on PSL matches. The depth of coverage varies by bookmaker, with Hollywoodbets and Betway generally offering the most PSL-specific markets.
