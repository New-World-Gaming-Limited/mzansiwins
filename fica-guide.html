FICA Verification at SA Betting Sites: The Full Guide

Every licensed South African betting site must verify your identity before it will pay out a single rand. This is not a policy choice. It's the law. FICA verification is required under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act, and skipping it means you simply can't withdraw. This guide explains exactly what FICA is, which documents you'll need, how long each major bookmaker takes to process your submission, and what to do when something goes wrong.

What Is FICA and Why Betting Sites Require It

FICA stands for the Financial Intelligence Centre Act No. 38 of 2001. The act requires certain businesses, including banks, lawyers, estate agents, and gambling operators, to verify the identity of their customers. The official term is Know Your Customer, or KYC. The goal is to prevent money laundering: criminals using gambling accounts to move dirty money through legitimate transactions.

For betting sites, this means they must collect and verify your identity documents before they release any funds. You can usually register, deposit, and bet without completing the process, but withdrawals are blocked until your account is fully verified. This applies without exception to every site holding a South African provincial gambling licence.

The Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) oversees compliance. Bookmakers that fail to verify customers face heavy fines and can lose their operating licences. That's why the process is non-negotiable: no FICA, no payout.

SA's FATF grey list (2023-2024): South Africa was added to the Financial Action Task Force grey list in February 2023, which flagged strategic weaknesses in the country's anti-money laundering framework. This prompted stricter enforcement at betting sites, leading to more document requests and slightly longer review times. SA exited the grey list in October 2024 after meeting the required action items. You may still notice more thorough checks at some operators as a result of that period.

All licensed operators listed on our betting sites page are required to follow FICA. If a site lets you withdraw without any identity verification, it's not operating under a South African licence, and your money has no regulatory protection. It's also worth noting that casino sites follow the same rules; see our casino sites hub for licensed operators.

Documents You'll Need

Most bookmakers require three or four document types. Get these ready before you start your upload. It's faster to submit everything in one session than to go back and forth.

  • Identity document
    South African green ID book, smart ID card, or a valid passport. Foreign passports are accepted at most licensed operators. Smart ID cards process fastest. For smart ID cards, upload both the front and back. Your name on the document must match your account registration exactly.
  • Proof of address
    Must show your full name, residential address, and be dated within the last 3 months. Accepted documents: utility bill (electricity, water, municipal services), official bank statement, municipal account, or a letter from SARS. A bank statement downloaded from your banking app works perfectly if you don't have a utility bill to hand.
  • Proof of banking
    A screenshot from your banking app showing your full name and account number, or a cancelled cheque. This confirms you own the bank account you'll use for withdrawals. The account name must match the name on your ID and betting account. For payment method-specific guidance, visit our payment methods hub, including details on EFT withdrawals and Ozow payments.
  • Selfie with ID (some sites only)
    A handful of operators ask you to take a photo of yourself holding your open ID document. This confirms you're the person in the ID photo. If required, shoot in good lighting, since the ID text must be readable in the photo.

Name matching matters most. Register with your name exactly as it appears on your identity document. Middle names, initials, and hyphenated surnames all need to match. A mismatch between your account name and your ID is the single most common reason for FICA delays, and some sites require you to contact support to correct it rather than fixing it yourself.

Step-by-Step FICA Verification

The process is broadly the same across all licensed SA bookmakers. A few operators have a slightly different menu structure, but these steps apply at almost every site.

  1. Log in to your account

    Open the app or website and sign in. Make sure your account details, especially your name and date of birth, match your identity document exactly before you start.

  2. Find the FICA or Verification section

    Go to your Profile or Account settings. Look for a tab or button labelled "FICA", "Verify Account", "Identity Verification", or "Documents". On mobile apps it's often under the profile icon in the top corner.

  3. Upload your identity document

    For a smart ID card, upload both the front and back. For a green ID book, a single clear photo of the open ID page is usually enough. Passports: the main identity page only. Ensure all four corners are visible and no text is cut off.

  4. Upload your proof of address

    A utility bill, bank statement, or municipal account dated within the last 3 months. The document must show your full name and home address. If you're using a bank statement, download it as a PDF from your banking app. Screenshot formats are accepted at most sites but PDF is preferred.

  5. Upload your proof of banking

    A screenshot from your banking app showing your name and account number. The account name must match your betting account. For more on which payment methods are available, see our payment methods guide.

  6. Take a selfie with your ID (if required)

    Some operators ask for a selfie holding your open ID. Hold the ID next to your face so both are clearly visible. Use good lighting and avoid bright windows behind you, which will silhouette your face.

  7. Wait for approval

    You'll typically get an email or SMS when your account is verified. Check your spam folder if you don't receive anything. If you haven't heard back after 72 hours, contact the bookmaker's support team directly with your account details.

Photo Tips for Fast Approval

Poor document photos are the single biggest cause of FICA rejections and delays. A few minutes spent getting the shot right can save you days of waiting. These are the most common reasons documents get rejected.

Blurry photo
Fix: Hold your phone steady or rest it on a flat surface. Tap the document on your screen to set focus before shooting. Use the rear camera, not the front-facing one.
Corners cropped
Fix: Place the document on a flat, plain-coloured surface. Back up until all four corners are visible in frame, then zoom in slightly if needed rather than physically moving closer.
Glare on laminated ID
Fix: Turn off your phone flash. Use natural light or an overhead lamp. Tilt the document slightly. Even a few degrees breaks the glare reflection completely.
File too large
Fix: Most sites cap uploads at 5MB. If your photo is too large, use your phone's native compression option or a free app like Squoosh. JPEG at 80% quality is typically fine for FICA purposes.
Expired document
Fix: Check the expiry date before uploading. An expired passport will be rejected. South African green ID books and smart IDs don't expire, but a driving licence does. Use your ID document rather than your licence.
Proof of address too old
Fix: Must be dated within the last 3 months. Download a bank statement from your banking app right now. It takes about 30 seconds and will be accepted by all major SA bookmakers.
Text not readable
Fix: Check that your ID number, name, and date of birth are all readable before uploading. If any text is obscured by shadow, reshoot with different lighting.
Wrong document type
Fix: A driving licence is not accepted as a primary identity document at most SA bookmakers. Use your green ID book, smart ID card, or passport instead.

Pro tip: Submit during business hours on a weekday. Verification teams typically work Monday to Friday, 8am to 5pm. A document uploaded at 9am on a Monday will almost always be reviewed faster than one submitted on a Friday afternoon or over the weekend.

Can I Bet Without Completing FICA?

Yes, in most cases. The majority of licensed SA bookmakers let you register, deposit, and place bets before your identity is verified. What you can't do is withdraw. Your account balance is effectively locked until FICA is approved.

Some operators set a deposit cap for unverified accounts, typically R1,000 to R5,000, before they block further deposits and require you to complete FICA. This varies by operator and isn't always published clearly, so it's best to start the verification process as soon as you register rather than waiting until you want to withdraw.

A few operators, particularly newer ones, have started requiring FICA before any deposit can be made. This is becoming more common as the FIC tightens enforcement. Check the specific site's terms when you register.

Don't wait until withdrawal day. Start your FICA submission when you register, not when you want to cash out. If you submit documents the moment you want to withdraw, you're adding 24 to 72 hours to your wait time. Getting verified upfront means your first withdrawal goes through without delay.

POPIA and Your Data

The Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), which came into full force in July 2021, sets out how organisations in South Africa must handle personal data. For bettors, this matters when you upload sensitive identity documents to a gambling site.

Under POPIA, licensed bookmakers must:

  • Tell you why they're collecting your documents and how they'll be used
  • Store your documents securely, with appropriate encryption and access controls
  • Only use your documents for the stated purpose of FICA compliance
  • Not share your documents with third parties without your consent, unless required by law (e.g., a court order or FIC reporting obligation)
  • Allow you to request access to or deletion of your personal data, subject to regulatory retention requirements

Licensed operators are required to keep identity documents on file for a period after account closure, typically five years, for regulatory audit purposes. They can't delete them immediately on your request during this retention window, but they must maintain security over the documents for the entire period.

If you have concerns about how a bookmaker handles your data, their privacy policy must be publicly available on their website. You can also lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator at inforegulator.org.za if you believe your POPIA rights have been violated.

Troubleshooting Common FICA Problems

Most FICA problems fall into a small number of categories. Here's what to do in each situation.

Your documents were rejected

Check the rejection email or notification carefully, as it will usually state which document failed and why. The most common reasons are blurry photos, documents with cut-off corners, a name mismatch between your account and ID, or a proof of address that's older than 3 months. Fix the specific issue, reshoot the document, and resubmit. There's no penalty for resubmitting, and most sites process resubmissions faster than first-time submissions.

Your name doesn't match your ID

Don't resubmit documents hoping the system will accept a mismatch. It won't. Contact the bookmaker's support team via live chat or email and explain the situation. Most operators can update your account name to match your ID if you can verify your account through another method (date of birth, ID number, registered email). Do this before uploading documents, not after.

You've been waiting more than 72 hours

Most SA bookmakers complete verification within 24 to 48 hours. If you've passed 72 hours without an update, contact support directly. Have your account username, the date you submitted documents, and your submission confirmation number ready if you have one. Live chat typically resolves this faster than email.

You don't have a utility bill

A bank statement downloaded directly from your banking app is accepted by every major SA bookmaker as proof of address. Log into your banking app, navigate to statements, download the most recent monthly statement as a PDF, and upload it. The process takes about two minutes and doesn't require printing or visiting a bank.

You keep getting asked to resubmit

If you've been asked to resubmit more than twice for the same document, escalate to a supervisor via live chat or ask the support agent to manually review your case. Include a note explaining previous rejections. Persistent problems often indicate a technical issue with the upload portal rather than a problem with your documents.

You can't find the FICA upload section

If you're struggling to locate the verification section in an app or website, search for "FICA" in the site's help section, or contact live chat and ask for a direct link. On most platforms it's under Profile, My Account, or Settings. Look for a verification badge or an "Incomplete" status indicator.
